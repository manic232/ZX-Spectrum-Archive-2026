Grand Prix Drivers

 

Created By:  Jonathan Cauldwell
Release Year: 1993

Not strictly a homebrew title, but it was never commercially released.


Grand Prix Drivers is a top-down racing game developed by Jonathan Cauldwell and published in 1993 for the ZX Spectrum 48K. Originally conceived as a conversion of a Commodore 64 game titled Jimmy's Grand Prix for Beyond Belief, the project was abandoned when the company folded. Cauldwell later revised the game and released it under the new name as part of his Bumper Boogie Pack compilation.


The game features competitive circuit racing with support for one or two players, utilizing joystick interfaces such as Interface2 and Kempston. It includes AY sound support and offers a classic racing experience with static top-down 2D graphics. Although the Commodore 64 version was never officially released, the Spectrum version has been preserved.
