Unclear Humour

CRASH Issue 19 - Aug 1985

 

CRASH Magazine Issue 19 (August 1985) featured a controversial four-page spoof titled "Unclear User," which mocked rival publication Sinclair User. The satire, which included a fake cover and articles accusing the rival magazine of accepting bribes for positive reviews and fabricating exclusives, led EMAP (publisher of Sinclair User) to obtain a legal injunction preventing the magazine's distribution. 

 The issue was recalled from newsagents and replaced with a stickered version with the offending pages removed, while an apology was published in the following issue. Despite the legal setback and an out-of-court settlement of approximately £60,000, the controversy generated significant publicity that helped CRASH double its monthly circulation from roughly 50,000 to over 100,000 copies.
