The Toy Box

This area focuses on the games I remember playing as a child in the 80s.

The Toy Box... an old wooden box made for me by my Grandpa when I was a very young child, I guess I would have kept toys in it at some point in my childhood, but in the 80s when I had my Spectrum, it was used as a platform to keep my Spectrum tapes on (including ones I'd copied using my Dad's Hi-Fi system). They would sit on top of the box eagerly waiting to be played.  I still have the Toy Box to this day.

I only ever owned a 48K Spectrum in the 80s, so there is no 128k titles here, to my mind the 128K games were simply the same 48K titles, just with better sound thanks to the AY-3-8912 sound chip that the 128k had.

I have attempted to list as many games as I can remember playing.  It's probably not complete, there are bound to be games that I played that I have forgotten now.

The list of games here link to CRASH reviews where possible (white) and my original instructions if no CRASH review is available (red).

My Memories

I had my Spectrum sat on a chest of draws, I didn't have a desk. I would select a game from the toy box lid, put it in the tape deck, type LOAD"" and patiently wait 2 or 3 minuets for it to load, excitedly anticipating what endless wonders the game would bring, only for the confounded thing to crash with the dreaded R Tape loading error, 0:1. In the early days I only had a black and white TV, that was bought for me to go with the ZX81 I had before the Spectrum. My parents had a portable colour TV in the dining room, and I used to pinch it at weekends, I remember my Mum calling up to me every Sunday night "BRING THAT TV DOWN NOW!" sometimes I did, but more often than not I... forgot! In the end I got my way and kept it in my room on a permanent basis.

My Grandmother (we called her Muz or Muzzy) used to come into my room sometimes after I told her about my latest game purchase, she would sit there watching the game as I excitedly showed her how to play it.  I can't imagine that she was the slightest bit interested, but she pretended for me!

We had no end of problems with the first tape deck we had, we took it back to Boots where we purchased it and had it replaced. In Hindsight I don't think there was a problem with it, just the usual problems with loading tape games back then. We went to my Cousin Justin's place soon after I got the Spectrum (he also had one) to show me how to use it, I remember him showing me some of the games that he had, one was River Rescue. I still love that game to this day.

Often school friends and family would play my games, I remember one time my Cousin Grant and Damien come to stay with us, and Grant really got into The Wild Bunch. He sat playing the game, trying to work out who the culprit was, I remember him making detailed notes in an attempt to beat the game.  Years later I recounted this story to him, but he had no memory of it.

One of the things that always amazed me as a child was when games included in-game speech, usually it was at the game's title screen, or at a key point in the game, such as completing a level.  I remember Ghostbusters, Robin of the Wood and Splat! having speech and it absolutely befogged me how it was done. Was it clever use of the beep command? some kind of early form of sampling?, witchcraft? who knows?, but I found it fascinating.

The games I remember playing most were Daley Thompson's Decathlon, River Rescue, Jet Set Willy, Manic Miner, Splat!, Alien 8, Knight Lore, Underwurlde, Wheelie and 3D Deathchase. I have a particular memory of having a dentist appointment one morning before school, and loading up 3D Deathchase for a quick game beforehand, presumably to take my mind off the horrors that were awaiting me on the dentist's chair!
