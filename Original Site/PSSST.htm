PSSST

 

 

Publisher: Ultimate Play The Game

 

Authors: Chris and Tim Stamper

 

Year of Release: 1983

 

Description: In a similar vein to Jetpac, Pssst has a pretty static screen. Your flower grows from the middle bottom of the screen upwards, and the nasties appear from the left and right hand side edges. You have a series of spray cans to choose from - each nasty responds to a different coloured can. And that's about it.

 

Overview: Robbie the robot, sits in his garden, fetching the odd bag of compost to help his prize Thyrgodian Megga Chrisanthodil grow. When suddenly, the whole garden is invaded by the most devilish, destructive insects you could imagine. But, Robbie is prepared!

 

 

 

 

 

Controls:

Keyboard Controls:

LEFT, Robot will move left by using the 'Q' key on the third row.
RIGHT, Robot will move right by using the 'W' key on the third row.
UP, Robot will move up by using the 'E' key on the third row.
DOWN, Robot will move down by using the 'R' key on the third row.
FIRE, Robot will fire the various spray cans by using the 'T' key on the third row.
PAUSE, The game may be paused by depressing the CAP SHIFT key on the first row.

 

 

Joystick Controls:

PSSST can be controlled using KEMPSTON Competition Pro Joysticks, please write for further details.

Instruction Manual

GROWING INSTRUCTIONS

Plant these seeds in good soil, growth can be speeded up by collecting all compost bags, keeping young shoots free from all garden pests and by looking after the leaves, which collect sunlight. Remember, the more leaves the plant has, the faster it will grow. As the young shoots give off an exotic aroma, the plant is especially prone to and will in fact attract hoards of Interstellar Space Slugs, Scuttling Leaches and Menacing Midges. Use of correct repellants will prevent all these attacks.

Please note: Only approximately 20% of all plants will blossom and reveal Robbie's Ultimate dream.

Growing time approximately 2 to 5 minutes.

Facts About the Game:

Pssst was among the first batch of games (others mentioned above) to come from the (what was to be) legendary software house.

Whilst other software companies were busy writing arcade conversions in very poor code and graphics. Ultimate were making arcade quality (at the time) games with bright, bold colours, great sound, and above all, GREAT GAMEPLAY.
