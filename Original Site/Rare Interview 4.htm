Rare Interview, Edge, Dec 1997
Page 4


Tim Stamper, managing director, and Chris Stamper, Technical Director

Edge: Let's start with this: why has Rare been so secretive over the years?

Tim Stamper: I guess we're not here for the personal publicity, we're here for the publicity of the company; rather than seeing an interview with a picture of somebody and a picture of the game, we're here to promote and push the videogame as far as we can.

Chris Stamper: I think another reason, as well, is that there just aren't enough hours in the day. We're still working ridiculous hours, seven days in the week, and I'd much rather focus on the games we're working on than spend time promoting ourselves. We've always taken that sort of approach.

Tim: We get people writing to us to do conferences and things like that and if we did all the conferences and all of the speeches I just don't know what we'd tell all these people, you know: "write good videogames, see you". [Laughs]

Chris: There's always something to do on the latest games that we're working on; there's always some details that we need to look at, to review, and when you're happy with one part, you just move on to the next, so it never ends, and I'm much happier getting involved with the games than with anything else.
