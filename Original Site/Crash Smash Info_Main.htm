CRASH Smashes

This part of the site focuses on the hundreds of games that received a 'CRASH Smash' review award by that much loved Spectrum mag of the 80's & early 90's, CRASH.

The list to the left are all those games, and they link to instructions and information on some of the games in the Games - CRASH Smashes folder.

Entries that are Greyed-Out indicate that the instructions/review (see below) is not available, or that the game is one of the Illusive games that do not seem to be available to download anywhere on the Internet.  Italic Greyed-Out link to an explanation of why the game is unavailable.

Cyan coloured links do not link to instructions, but do link to a review, preview or other relevant material on the Sinclair User, Your Spectrum, CRASH or Your Sinclair web site (obviously an active internet connection must be open to use these).

Some of the instructions main headings (Jet Set Willy for example) are also coloured Cyan.  They indicate that they also link to relevant material (reviews etc) from the aforementioned sites above (again an open internet connection must be active).  The only exception is the Ultimate Play The Game titles, the headings for these link to the relevant section of the Ultimate Wurlde site and are not coloured Cyan.

Note: There are some instructions that I have not formatted nicely, due to the size of the original text document.  Where this is the case I have made sure to to highlight at the top of the page under the main title.  There are also a few cases where the font is not in the nice Verdana that you see here, due to formatting/display issues.

An Introduction to CRASH

CRASH (Feb 1984 - April 1992) was the youngest and shortest-lived of the three main Sinclair magazines. It occupied a rather different niche from its principal rivals, Sinclair User and Your Spectrum (later Your Sinclair). Whereas SU aimed itself at the "serious" Sinclair user and YS at the techies, CRASH was targeted from the start at the gamers, as its "loud" title suggested.

This was a smart choice. Although the other two magazines had a solid niche following, CRASH's founders recognised from the start that by far the biggest group of Spectrum users were gamers first and foremost. A similar approach was adopted for CRASH's sister magazines, ZZAP!64 (for the Commodore 64) and AMTIX (for the Amstrad CPC), and proved highly successful on all three titles. CRASH had the largest circulation of the three major Sinclair magazines (over 100,000 copies a month at its peak) and was the standard guide for the software distribution trade in deciding which games to stock in the shops. The magazine was, however, the first to succumb to market forces and bit the dust in the summer of 1992; its rivals lasted another year before they too died.

So what's with all this 'CRASH Smash' stuff then?  Every month CRASH awarded a few chosen games the CRASH Smash review award.  Basically this meant that the game was of a quality sufficient enough to warrant it.  I guess the modern day equivalent would be a game that received 90% and over.  Strangely there were a few game that got an overall score of below 90% but somehow still got the CRASH Smash, don't ask me how they worked that out!!
