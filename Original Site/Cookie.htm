Cookie

 

 

Publisher: Ultimate Play The Game

 

Authors: Chris and Tim Stamper

 

Year of Release: 1983

 

Description: Cookie is a type of what could only be described as a shoot-em-up. The object of the game is to mix ingredients to bake a cake.

 

Overview: Move Charlie the Chef around the screen throwing bags of flour at the ingredients in an attempt to knock them into your mixing bowl at the bottom of the screen. Avoid knocking nuts and bolts e.t.c in the bowl as the number of ingredients you have to place in the bowl before you can move to the next level will increase.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Controls:

Keyboard Controls:

LEFT, Charlie will move left by using the 'Q' key.
RIGHT, Charlie will move right by using the 'W' key.
DOWN, Charlie will move down by using the 'E' key.
UP, Charlie will move up by using the 'R' key.
FIRE, Charlie will throw flour bags when the 'T' key is pressed.
PAUSE, the game by using the CAP SHIFT key.

 

Joystick Controls:

COOKIE can be controlled using either Kempston or Protek/ cursor controlled joysticks by selecting the correct mode.

As soon as they can, the ingredients dash out of the pantry dragging all sorts of nasties, found in the bottom of drawers and cupboards, with them, to escape and run wild. Poor old Charlie! he has to daze the ingredients with his flour bombs and knock them into the mixing bowl, because if they go into the dustbins they will be eaten by the bin monster, who doesn't care one little bit where he throws any of his rubbish. Should any rubbish or nasties get into the cake mix then the "ingredients needed" counter will change, and Charlie will have to work quicker and faster, if he's going to bake his cake.

Facts About the Game:

Cookie was among the first batch of games released by Ultimate at the small price of 5.95. The other games released then were Jetpac, Transam, and Pssst.

 

Sequels/Prequels:

Although Charlie the Chef was a great little character with bags of personality he never appeared in any other games.

 

Notes:

The first set of computer games produced by Chris and Tim Stamper after they moved away from the amusement machine designing, deciding there was no money to be made in it. Hypocrites!
