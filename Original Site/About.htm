About This Site

So, what's this site all about then?  Well, put simply I have been a ZX Spectrum fanatic ever since owning my first Spectrum back in the early eighties (the nasty rubber keyed one!).  It was brought for me by my parents one Christmas and I can even remember the first game I played on it on that Christmas night, Horace Goes Skiing.

The ZX Spectrum is an absolute classic retro machine, many of the games are still very playable today (Alien 8 for example is as playable today as it was when a school friend of mine first copied it to a tape for me!).  A lot of Speccy games are very hard indeed, well they had to be, there was not enough memory in the machine to create loads of levels, so the developers just made the games really hard in the hope it would last longer!!  Back then gameplay was the most important consideration when creating a game, graphics were not the be all and end all like they are now, because they couldn't be, the machines just were not up to it.  that is the reason that I think the Spectrum still has the following it has now as it had when it first came out, the games are still very playable (if not very pretty to look at) and is the main inspiration for me creating this site and collecting games.

But anyway...

After recently discovering the excellent eBay auction site and seeing that I could get hold of a second hand Spectrum for about £20-£25, I wasted no time in bidding for one.  As well as buying a Spectrum +2, I have also been collecting rather a lot of games and am proud to own a number of Ultimate titles.  Seeing those good old loading screens after all those years brought the old nostalgia flooding back, so I decided to bring together as many games, instructions, covers etc... as I could from Holy Grail of Spectrum emulation sites World of Spectrum.  I did use a few other sites as well as getting the WOS CD Rom from Craig Rothwell, to fill this collection.

These pages were started in December 2001 and are designed to complement my growing collection of the aforementioned files.  I use the superb ZX32 Spectrum emulator for the PC.  See Help for more information on this gem of an emulator.

So what's here? well, Instructions for some of the games in the archive can be found within these pages, as well as history, interviews, web links and loads more (see below for more information).  There are several sections to the site.  They can be accessed via the main menu frame at the top of the page.  Here is a brief description of each section...

  • ZX Spectrum Archive Home: This is the home page of the archive, it has general information archive on the ZX Spectrum, as well as history, facts & figures and various web links.  These links are (in my opinion) the best Spectrum related sites on the net.  there are loads more out there, but theses are the ones I like best.
  • CRASH Smashes: Contains a complete list and some info on the games that were awarded a 'CRASH Smash' review award by everyone's favourite Speccy mag, CRASH.  There are missing titles from this section, click here for more.
  • Ultimate Play The Game: This is an area concentrating solely on Spectrum games written by the legendary developer Ultimate Play The Game.  Why does it get it's own area? well they were by far the best (and most successful) game developer for the Spectrum.  The games they wrote were far ahead of anything else being written at the time, but most importantly they were very playable, so I decided to give them the credit they deserve by giving them a separate section.
  • My Games: Here I have attempted to bring together all the games I remember owning as a child.  Such classics as Manic Miner, Jet Set Willy to name but two.
  • Pokes One/Two: Thousands upon thousands of Pokes to use with the games in the Archive, Infinite lives, maximum energy etc.  See Help to learn how to use them.
  • About: The text you are reading now ;o)
  • Help: General help area for using this site, contents of the folders and a brief introduction to the ZX32 emulator.

So, what are you waiting for? get playing those old games and re-live your childhood all over again!! Don't forget to eat every once in a while though... oh and you will need to use the toilet too... playing these old games will make you forget, believe me!!

You may wish to take a look at the Help section after reading this for more information on using the this site and the ZX32 emulator, as well as information on what is in the folders.
